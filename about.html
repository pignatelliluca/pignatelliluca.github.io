About Me

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Calculus of Variations at Radboud University Nijmegen, under the supervision of Prof. Riccardo Cristoferi. My position runs from September 2023 to August 2027.

My doctoral research focuses on Homogenisation and Phase Separation with multiple microscales. I study the asymptotic behaviour, via \(\Gamma\)-convergence, of Cahn–Hilliard-type energies in heterogeneous periodic media when two (or more) length scales \(\varepsilon \ll \delta \ll 1\) compete simultaneously. The goal is to identify the effective sharp-interface limit and understand how the geometry of the microstructure governs the resulting surface tension.

The central variational object is a family of functionals \[ \mathcal{F}_{\varepsilon,\delta}[u] = \int_\Omega \left[\frac{1}{\varepsilon} W\!\left(\tfrac{x}{\delta},\, u(x)\right) + \varepsilon\,|\nabla u(x)|^2\right]dx, \] where \(W(y,\cdot)\) is a double-well potential that is \(\mathbb{Z}^n\)-periodic in \(y\). As \(\varepsilon,\delta\to 0\), depending on the relative rate \(\varepsilon/\delta\), the \(\Gamma\)-limit takes qualitatively different forms and the effective surface tension is determined by a cell problem encoding the microscale geometry.

Before Nijmegen I completed a Master's degree in Engineering Mathematics at the Polytechnic University of Turin, with a thesis on quasi-static brittle fracture in the Francfort–Marigo model, supervised by Prof. Marco Morandotti. During my Master's I spent an exchange semester at KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm.

A Favourite Result

Theorem (Modica & Mortola, 1977)

Let \(\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^n\) be open and bounded. The functionals \[ \mathcal{F}_\varepsilon[u] = \int_\Omega \left(\varepsilon|\nabla u|^2 + \frac{W(u)}{\varepsilon}\right)dx \] \(\Gamma\)-converge as \(\varepsilon\to 0\), in \(L^1(\Omega)\), to a multiple of the perimeter functional \(c_W \,\mathcal{H}^{n-1}(\partial\{u=1\}\cap\Omega)\), where \(c_W = 2\int_0^1\!\sqrt{W(s)}\,ds\).
