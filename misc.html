Recently Read

A running list of books I've read or am reading — math and otherwise.

Bra

Functional Analysis, Calculus of Variations and Optimal Control

Francis Clarke

A rigorous and elegant treatment that bridges functional analysis and variational methods. The chapter on necessary conditions is especially well written.

Dal

An Introduction to \(\Gamma\)-Convergence

Gianni Dal Maso

The classic reference. Dense but rewarding — I keep coming back to it.

Bra

Lectures on the Calculus of Variations and Optimal Control Theory

L. C. Young

Charming and idiosyncratic. Young's enthusiasm for the subject is infectious.

Umb

Il Nome della Rosa

Umberto Eco

Re-reading in the original Italian. The layered references and the monastery as an epistemic labyrinth — better every time.

Thoughts

Occasional short reflections. Not a blog — more like margin notes.

March 2026

There's something deeply satisfying about \(\Gamma\)-convergence: the idea that a messy sequence of functionals can converge, in a meaningful sense, to something clean. It's a kind of mathematical patience — trust the limit.

November 2024

Giving the PhD colloquium talk on Plateau's problem reminded me why I love mathematics outreach. The question "why do soap films minimise area?" is elementary to state, yet opens into one of the richest chapters of 20th-century analysis.
